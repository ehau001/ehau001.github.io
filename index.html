As a business major at the University of California, Riverside (UCR), combining academic pursuits with volunteer work offers a multifaceted approach to personal and professional development. The curriculum in the School of Business Administration at UCR provides a comprehensive understanding of various aspects of business management, including finance, marketing, entrepreneurship, and organizational behavior. Through rigorous coursework and practical experiences, students gain the knowledge and skills necessary to thrive in diverse business environments. Moreover, UCR's emphasis on innovation and collaboration fosters an environment where students are encouraged to think critically, solve complex problems, and adapt to the ever-changing demands of the global marketplace.

Engaging in volunteer work alongside academic endeavors as a business major at UCR offers invaluable opportunities for personal growth and community impact. Volunteering allows students to apply theoretical concepts learned in the classroom to real-world situations, enhancing their understanding of social responsibility and ethical decision-making in business contexts. Whether participating in community service projects, serving on nonprofit boards, or assisting local businesses with strategic initiatives, students develop leadership, communication, and teamwork skills that complement their academic studies. Additionally, volunteering provides a sense of fulfillment and purpose as students contribute to the betterment of society while gaining practical experience and expanding their professional networks.

Participating in a business internship provides invaluable hands-on experience and insights into the workings of the corporate world for students pursuing their academic journey. During an internship, business majors at the University of California, Riverside (UCR), have the opportunity to apply theoretical knowledge gained in the classroom to real-world scenarios, bridging the gap between academia and industry. Whether interning at a multinational corporation, a startup, or a nonprofit organization, students gain practical skills, develop professional networks, and cultivate a deeper understanding of their chosen field. Through mentorship, project assignments, and exposure to various departments, interns at UCR gain firsthand exposure to different facets of business operations, including marketing, finance, human resources, and strategic planning.
